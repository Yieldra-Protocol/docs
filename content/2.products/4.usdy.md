USDY is the decentralized, overcollateralized stablecoin of the Yieldra ecosystem. Unlike traditional lending models where borrowing rates fluctuate unpredictably, USDY introduces a new level of both **certainty and flexibility** for users, depending on what the user is after.

USDY is minted by providing liquidity into the specialized USDY module, with both **fixed-term** and **variable-term** borrowing options determined by the DAO. This makes USDY one of the most adaptable stablecoins in DeFi, giving users choice while ensuring transparency in the cost of borrowing.

## Key Features

### 1. Overcollateralized & Decentralized

- Every USDY token is backed by excess collateral deposited into the USDY module
- No reliance on centralized issuers, issuance and redemption are governed by smart contracts and DAO oversight

### 2. Fixed & Variable Interest Options

- **Fixed Term Borrowing**: Users lock in a guaranteed interest rate for predictable costs over time for a set period of time
- **Variable Term Borrowing**: Users access more flexible borrowing tied to market conditions for any amount of time
- Both models are set and adjusted by the **Yieldra DAO**, balancing stability with adaptability

### 3. Certainty in Lending

One of the major issues in DeFi today is that **lending rates offer no certainty to borrowers or lenders**. Yieldra solves this by introducing fixed-rate borrowing alongside variable options, giving users clear expectations while still leaving room for market dynamics.

### 4. DAO-Governed Monetary Policy

Rates, collateral parameters, and issuance rules are controlled by the Yieldra DAO. This ensures a **transparent, community-driven monetary policy** for USDY.

### 5. USDY <> USDT Conversions

- Users can convert USDT directly into USDY at any time for a small fee
- Users can convert USDY back into USDT (provided there is sufficient liquidity) for a small fee

## Why It Matters

Most decentralized stablecoins struggle with either **volatility in borrowing costs** or **centralized control**. USDY offers the best of both worlds: **decentralization, overcollateralization, and predictable rates**.

By letting users choose between fixed and variable borrowing, Yieldra provides certainty where it's needed while maintaining flexibility for those who want it.
